About Me

  I went back to school in 2016 to pursue an education in Computer Science. After receiving an associate degree from JCCC, I was accepted to the University of Kansas and enrolled in the Computer Science undergraduate program. Under many great professors such as Dr. John Gibbons, Dr. Andrew Gill, and Dr. Drew Davidson, I became proficient at programming as well as the science behind it. In 2020, I had the opportunity to work for the up-and-coming startup Bixy where I helped document and review code, onboard new interns, and develop additional app functionality.

 Through my learning experiences at the University of Kansas in Compiler Construction, Programming Languages, and Software Engineering, I have been exposed to many uses of programming. From making a computer language using flex, bison, and C++ to the structure and usage of functional programming. It was through such classes that I developed an enjoyment and expertise in backend development. This has also furthered my love for solving problems and finding optimizations.
